About Advanced Signal Processing and Machine Intelligence Research (ASPMIR)

Advanced Signal Processing and Machine Intelligence Research (ASPMIR) group explores theoretical foundations of signal processing, machine intelligence and knowledge from relevant engineering and scientific domains to develop state-of-the-art artifacts (i.e. algorithms, models, architecture, testbeds and products) for telecommunication, biomedical, agricultural and education sectors. ASPMIR group has collaboration with scholars in academic laboratories and departments at the Kwara State University, Ilorin, Nigeria; University of Uyo, Uyo, Nigeria; Institute for System Science at the Durban University of Technology (DUT), Durban, South Africa; Information and Communication Technology Department, Mongoshutu University of Technology (MUT), Durban, South Africa; and University of Texas at Austin, United States of America (USA).

Members of the group have benefited from research, development, teaching and travel grants from Nigeria Communication Commission (NCC); Tertiary Education Trust Fund (TETFUND), Nigeria; Google, USA; National Research Foundation (NRF), South Africa; Covenant University, Nigeria; Durban University of Technology (DUT), South Africa; National Science Foundation (NSF), USA; World Bank, France Development Agency (AFD), United Bank of Africa (UBA), Nigeria; the Rockefeller Foundation, USA; and host of others.
